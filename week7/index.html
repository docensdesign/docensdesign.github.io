Making the impossible – possible

For the past thirty years, Doris Salcedo's work has focused solely on representing the effects of political violence – without recreating violence. She says her work is like a funeral oration, for the past thirty years she has been immersed in mourning and her work is the act of mourning.

Salcedo brings emotion into the work through a combination of materials and process to create powerful visual metaphors that enable us to connect with the issues presented.

Political voilence is not a third world problem – Racism, oppression and violence are universal.

Salcedo creates a world in between the everyday and memory, where the impossible idea of honoring the dead and restoring their dignity becomes possible through imagination. Imagination is ignited by symbolism and acts as portal to realizing empathy for the victims, communities and families affected by political violence.
